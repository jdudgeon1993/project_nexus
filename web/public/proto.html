Phase-based redesign

Same single-page app, three states. Palette pulled directly from the live build (slate-950 base, sky-400 = dir 0 / primary, purple-400 = dir 1, emerald = active/on-time, amber = alerts/delay).

Phase 1Map idle

  • Sheet sits at 14% — just a hint "Search above or tap the map".
  • Floating search bar + ⚠️3 alert badge sit on the map (as today).
  • Type "A", "15L", or "Peoria" → live quick-results dropdown appears under search. Tap one.

Phase 2Route selected → sheet rises

  • Sheet animates to 52% (mid snap), header fills in: route pill + "A Line · To Denver Airport".
  • Direction toggle (sky for dir 0 / purple for dir 1) + Schedule/Directions/Alerts tabs appear — same structure as RailLineSection.tsx today.
  • Tap the grip again (or drag, simulated by tap) → sheet goes full (92%), search bar/alert badge fade out since they're now redundant.
  • Tap a stop card to expand inline detail (live status, transfers) — no extra navigation.

Phase 3Map reflects selection

  • The moment a route is picked, the map's route line switches from neutral slate to sky-400 (dir 0) with a glow, dir-1 line fades in as dashed purple.
  • Live vehicle markers (🚆) fade in and "move" along the line (try switching direction — markers reposition).
  • Tapping a stop on the map (try the dots) jumps the sheet to that stop, expanded.

MiscFAB = Alerts / Plan / Settings

  • Alerts now lives in the FAB and as a sheet tab — tapping FAB Alerts jumps straight to that tab at full height.
  • "Back to map" FAB item appears once the sheet is mid/full, collapsing it back to 14% (Phase 1) — this is the missing "exit" affordance from the current build.
  • Alerts tab keeps the search + category pills + clickable route badges from the real alerts work.

Try it

  • Type in the search bar → pick "A Line"
  • Drag/tap the grip to go full height
  • Switch direction (To Union Station) — watch the map markers swap color/position
  • Tap "Alerts" tab or FAB → search "Peoria", click a route badge
  • Tap "Back to map" to return to Phase 1
